Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about collision repair, insurance, and working with Tradewind Auto Body.

No. In Hawaii, you have the legal right to choose your own repair facility. Insurance companies may suggest shops on their Direct Repair Program, but you are not obligated to use them. We work with all major carriers regardless of their preferred shop lists.

Yes, all estimates at Tradewind Auto Body are completely free. We'll assess your vehicle, document the damage, and provide a detailed written estimate at no charge. If you're filing an insurance claim, we can also communicate directly with your adjuster on your behalf.

Yes. We work directly with all major insurance carriers including GEICO, State Farm, Allstate, USAA, Progressive, Farmers, and local Hawaii carriers. We handle the paperwork and follow up on approvals so you don't have to.

Yes. We prioritize OEM parts for all structural and safety-critical components to maintain your vehicle's factory specifications and safety ratings. For non-structural cosmetic components, we'll discuss your options and the cost difference between OEM and quality aftermarket alternatives.

Repair time depends on damage severity and parts availability. Minor repairs take 1–3 days, single-panel repairs 3–5 days, and moderate collision damage 1–2 weeks. Island shipping adds 3–5 days for parts from mainland suppliers. We'll give you a realistic timeline upfront and update you if anything changes.

Yes. All collision repairs at Tradewind Auto Body are covered by a limited lifetime warranty on workmanship as long as you own the vehicle. Paint work is warranted against peeling, cracking, and color fade for 5 years. Parts warranties are provided by the manufacturer.

Yes. We use computerized color-matching technology and factory paint codes to achieve precise color matches. We also blend adjacent panels when necessary to ensure a seamless, invisible repair — even on newer vehicles with complex metallic or pearl finishes.

Yes. We can coordinate rental vehicle arrangements for covered insurance claims and will work with your rental coverage to make the process as smooth as possible. Most insurance policies include rental coverage — check your policy or ask us when you bring your vehicle in.
