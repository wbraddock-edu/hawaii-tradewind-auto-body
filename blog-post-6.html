How Long Does Collision Repair Actually Take?

Editorial Staff · January 2026

One of the most common questions we hear at Tradewind Auto Body is: "How long will my car be in the shop?" It's completely understandable — you need your vehicle, and uncertainty about timing is stressful. We believe in honest, transparent communication, so here's a realistic breakdown of repair timelines by damage type.

The Variables That Affect Repair Time

Before diving into specific estimates, understand that several factors influence every repair: severity of damage, availability of parts (which is slower in Hawaii due to shipping), insurance approval timing, hidden damage discovered during teardown, and current shop workload. Every estimate we give includes these caveats — and we'll update you immediately if anything changes.

Minor Cosmetic Repairs: 1–3 Days

Small dents without paint damage, paintless dent repair, bumper scuffs, minor scratches, and single-panel touch-up work typically takes 1–3 business days. These repairs don't require structural inspection, insurance supplements, or extended parts ordering. Many minor repairs can be estimated and completed within the same week you bring your vehicle in.

Single Panel Collision Damage: 3–5 Days

Damage limited to one or two panels — a dented quarter panel, a damaged door, a bent bumper cover — typically takes 3–5 business days. This accounts for parts ordering (2–3 days from mainland suppliers to Maui), paint mixing and application (including cure time between coats), and blending adjacent panels for a seamless color match.

Moderate Collision Damage: 1–2 Weeks

When a collision involves structural components — bent frame rails, damaged unibody sections, deployed airbags, or multiple panel replacements — expect 1–2 weeks. Frame straightening on our computerized measuring system takes time to get right, and parts ordering for structural components must be completed before reassembly can begin.

Major Collision Damage: 2–4 Weeks

Severe front or rear impacts that require extensive teardown, structural repair, multiple subassembly replacements, and ADAS calibration (cameras, sensors, radar) require 2–4 weeks. If your vehicle's airbags deployed, every airbag module, sensor, and pretensioner must be replaced — and the entire safety system recalibrated before delivery.

Hawaii-Specific Delays

Living on an island adds genuine complexity to parts procurement. Parts shipped from mainland suppliers typically arrive in 3–5 business days by air freight, or 1–2 weeks by ocean freight for larger items. When possible, we order parts proactively while insurance approval is being processed — this significantly reduces overall cycle time.

What You Can Do to Speed Things Up

Report your claim to insurance immediately after the accident, authorize us to communicate directly with your adjuster, and provide any requested documentation promptly. At Tradewind Auto Body, we handle insurance documentation on your behalf and follow up proactively on approval delays. We provide rental vehicle coordination and update you at every stage of your repair.

We know you need your car. We work as efficiently as possible without cutting corners — because a repair done right the first time is always faster than doing it twice.
