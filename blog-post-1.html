What To Do After a Car Accident in Hawaii

Editorial Staff · June 2026

Being involved in a car accident is stressful anywhere — but in Hawaii, there are specific steps you should take to protect yourself legally, financially, and physically. Whether you're on the H-1 freeway in Honolulu or a winding road on Maui's Hana Highway, the moments immediately after a collision matter enormously.

Step 1: Prioritize Safety First

Move vehicles out of traffic if safely possible and turn on hazard lights. Hawaii law requires you to stop and remain at the scene of any accident involving injury, death, or property damage. Leaving the scene is a criminal offense in Hawaii under HRS §291C-12.

Step 2: Call 911 for Any Injuries

If anyone is injured — even if they say they're fine — call 911 immediately. Adrenaline masks pain, and symptoms like whiplash can appear hours or days later. Emergency services on Oahu respond through the Honolulu Police Department, while Maui, Kauai, and the Big Island each have county police departments.

Step 3: File a Police Report

Hawaii requires a written report to the county police department within 24 hours if the accident involves injury or more than $3,000 in property damage. Even for minor fender-benders, a police report provides crucial documentation for insurance claims.

Step 4: Exchange Information

Collect the other driver's name, contact information, driver's license number, vehicle registration, and insurance details. Take photos of vehicle damage, license plates, the scene, and any visible injuries. Note road and weather conditions.

Step 5: Notify Your Insurance Company

Report the accident to your insurance company as soon as possible. Hawaii is a no-fault insurance state, meaning your Personal Injury Protection (PIP) coverage pays for your medical expenses regardless of who caused the accident — up to $10,000 per person. However, property damage claims go through the at-fault driver's liability insurance.

Step 6: Choose Your Auto Body Shop Carefully

Here's what most people don't realize: you have the legal right to choose your own repair shop in Hawaii. Your insurance company may recommend certain shops, but you are not required to use them. Choose a shop that is I-CAR certified, uses OEM or approved parts, and has experience with your vehicle's make and model. At Tradewind Auto Body, we work directly with all major insurance carriers and handle the paperwork on your behalf.

Maui-Specific Considerations

If your accident occurs on remote roads like Hana Highway or Piilani Highway, getting a cellular signal for emergency calls can be difficult. Download offline maps before any island road trip and know the locations of the nearest hospitals — Maui Memorial Medical Center in Wailuku is the island's main trauma facility.

Getting into an accident is never pleasant, but knowing these steps ensures you protect your rights and get your vehicle properly restored. Our team at Tradewind Auto Body is here to guide you through every step of the repair process.
