How to Choose an Auto Body Shop on Maui

Editorial Staff · March 2026

Your car has just been in a collision. The insurance company is calling, recommending one of their "preferred" shops, and you're not sure what to do. Choosing the right auto body shop is one of the most important decisions you'll make after an accident — and knowing what to look for makes all the difference.

I-CAR Certification Matters

The Inter-Industry Conference on Auto Collision Repair (I-CAR) sets the standard for technical training in our industry. Look for shops with I-CAR Gold Class certification — this means technicians complete ongoing training to stay current with rapidly evolving vehicle technology, from advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) to high-strength steel construction. Tradewind Auto Body is I-CAR certified, and we invest in continuous training for every technician.

OEM vs. Aftermarket Parts

After a collision, your vehicle should be repaired with Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) parts whenever possible. OEM parts are made to your vehicle's exact specifications and maintain safety system integrity — particularly important for airbag sensors, crumple zones, and structural components. Ask any shop directly: "Do you use OEM or aftermarket parts?" and get the answer in writing in your repair estimate.

Your Right to Choose Your Shop

Hawaii law protects your right to choose your own repair facility. Insurance companies can recommend shops on their "Direct Repair Program" (DRP), but they cannot legally require you to use them. DRP shops work at negotiated rates with insurance companies — which can sometimes mean cost-cutting on parts or labor that isn't in your best interest. You're the customer; choose the shop you trust.

Ask the Right Questions

When evaluating a shop, ask: How long have you been in business? Do you provide a written warranty on repairs? Are your technicians ASE and I-CAR certified? Do you perform post-repair calibration for ADAS sensors? Can you show me examples of similar repair work? A reputable shop welcomes these questions — it's a sign that you're an informed consumer.

Read Reviews from Real Customers

Google, Yelp, and Facebook reviews give you unfiltered feedback from previous customers. Look beyond the star rating — read the narrative reviews for details about communication, timeliness, and whether the finished work matched expectations. A pattern of complaints about delays or poor color matching is a red flag regardless of the overall rating.

Get Multiple Estimates

For any repair over $500, get 2–3 written estimates. Compare not just the bottom line, but what's included: parts quality, labor hours, and whether sublet work (like glass replacement) is included. The lowest estimate isn't always the best value.

Why Maui Needs Specialized Knowledge

Maui's environment creates repair challenges unique to island vehicles. Salt-embedded rust around damaged areas must be completely treated before painting or it will bubble back through in months. Our technicians are trained specifically in tropical climate repair techniques — something mainland-trained shops may not appreciate.

You've worked hard for your vehicle. Choosing a qualified, honest repair shop ensures your car comes back better than ever — and stays that way in Hawaii's demanding environment.
